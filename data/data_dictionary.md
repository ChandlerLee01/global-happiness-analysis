# World Happiness Report 2023 – Extended Data Dictionary

## Core and Regression Model Variables

### ladder

Cantril life evaluation ('life ladder') — respondents rate their current lives on a scale from 0 (worst possible) to 10 (best possible).

### log GDP per capita

Natural logarithm of GDP per person (PPP-adjusted, constant 2017 international dollars).

### social_support

Binary indicator from: 'Do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you out?' (1 = yes, 0 = no).

### healthy_life_expectancy

Expected years of life in good health at birth, from WHO data (interpolated and extrapolated).

### freedom_to_make_life_choices

Average response to: 'Are you satisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?'

### generosity

Residual of per‑capita donations to charity after controlling for log GDP per capita.

### perceptions_of_corruption

Average perception of corruption in government and business.

### dystopia_and_residual

Benchmark of minimum values plus unexplained residual, used in decomposition models.

### Explained by: Log GDP per capita

This is one of the six key variables used to explain the variation in happiness scores across countries in the regression model.

### Explained by: Social support

Reflects national average of binary responses to whether people have someone to rely on in times of need.

### Explained by: Healthy life expectancy

Derived from WHO estimates; interpolated and extrapolated to align with the study years.

### Explained by: Freedom to make life choices

Uses Gallup's national average response to satisfaction with freedom.

### Explained by: Generosity

Based on residuals of national donation behavior after controlling for GDP.

### Explained by: Perceptions of corruption

Reflects perceived prevalence of corruption in government and business.

## Additional Emotional Variables

### positive_affect

Average of responses to whether respondent experienced laughter, enjoyment, and finding things interesting 'yesterday'.

### negative_affect

Average of responses to whether respondent experienced worry, sadness, and anger 'yesterday'.
